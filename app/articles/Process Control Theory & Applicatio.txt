Process Control Theory & Application
1.0: Title

Process Control Theory & Application

HRDCorp Claimable Training Program Approval No:   10001360296


2.0: Introduction

Process Control Theory & Application is a 2-day course designed to provide participants with a comprehensive understanding of the principles and practices of process control. The course is designed for engineers, operators, and technicians who work in process industries such as petrochemicals plants, power plants, pharmaceutical plants and so on.

3.0 Course Objectives and Outcome:

Upon completion of the program, participants should be able to:

To provide participants with a comprehensive understanding of the principles and practices of process control.
To help participants understand the behaviour of a process over time and how to model and identify process dynamics.
To teach participants the different strategies for controlling a process and how to implement and optimize a process control system.
To provide participants with the skills and knowledge needed to troubleshoot process control problems and improve process performance.
To provide participants with real-world examples of process control applications in various industries.
4.0 Course Contents

Day 1:

Session 1: Introduction to Process Control Theory

– Overview of process control and its importance in various industries

– Types of process control systems and their applications

– Understanding the process dynamics and their impact on control

Session 2: Process Modelling and Identification

– Introduction to process modelling and its importance in control design

– Types of process models and their applications

– Methods for process identification using experimental data

Session 3: Process Control Design

– Overview of control design methods, such as PID, feedback, feedforward model-based control

– Understanding the control design process, including selection of control strategy, tuning of controller parameters, and validation of the control system

Session 4: Process Monitoring and Diagnosis

– Importance of process monitoring and diagnosis in process control

– Techniques for process monitoring, such as statistical process control and process fault detection

– Methods for process diagnosis, such as fault isolation and root cause analysis

Day 2:

Session 1: Process Optimization and Improvement

– Introduction to process optimization and improvement

– Techniques for process optimization, such as process simulation and optimization

– Methods for process improvement, such as process redesign and process improvement methodologies

Session 2: Process Safety and Security

– Importance of process safety and security in process control

– Techniques for process safety, such as hazard analysis and risk assessment

– Methods for process security, such as cybersecurity and process safety management

Session 3: Process Integration and Collaboration

– Importance of process integration and collaboration in process control

– Techniques for process integration, such as process modelling and simulation

– Methods for process collaboration, such as process collaboration platforms and process collaboration methodologies

Session 4: Process Control Case Studies and Practical Examples

– Real-world examples of process control applications in various industries

– Case studies of successful process control implementations

– Discussion of practical challenges and solutions in process control

5.0 Who should attend

Plant Engineers, Plant Operation Personnel’s, and Technicians

6.0 Training Methodology & Certification

The course will be delivered through a combination of lectures, group discussions, and practical exercises to ensure a comprehensive understanding of process control theory and application. Participants will receive a certificate of completion at the end of the course.
