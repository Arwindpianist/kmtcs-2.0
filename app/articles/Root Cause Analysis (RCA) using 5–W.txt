Root Cause Analysis (RCA) using 5–WHY Method

2.0 Introduction

Root Cause Analysis (RCA) is a problem-solving technique used to identify the underlying reasons behind a particular issue or event. The 5 WHY method is a popular RCA technique that involves asking “why” five times to uncover the root cause of a problem. This 2-day course on Root Cause Analysis using the 5 WHY method aims to provide participants with the knowledge and skills required to effectively apply this technique in their work.

3.0 Course Objectives

Understanding the concept of root cause analysis and its importance in problem-solving
Learning the 5 WHY technique, a systematic approach to identifying the root cause of a problem
Understanding the difference between symptoms and root causes
Learning how to ask effective why questions to uncover the root cause
Understanding the importance of involving stakeholders in the root cause analysis process
Learning how to develop effective corrective actions based on the root cause analysis
Understanding the importance of verifying the effectiveness of corrective actions
Learning how to continuously improve processes by implementing corrective actions and monitoring results.
