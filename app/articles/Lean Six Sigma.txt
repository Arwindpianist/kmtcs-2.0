2.0 Introduction

Lean Six Sigma is a powerful methodology that combines the principles of Lean and Six Sigma to help organizations improve efficiency, reduce waste, and enhance customer satisfaction. This 2-days Lean Six Sigma course aims to provide participants with a comprehensive understanding of the Lean Six Sigma methodology, which combines the principles of Lean and Six Sigma to improve business processes and reduce waste.

3.0 Course Objectives

Understanding the Lean Six Sigma methodology and its benefits
Learning the DMAIC (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control) process improvement framework
Understanding the role of a Lean Six Sigma Green Belt and how to apply the methodology to improve processes
Learning how to identify and eliminate waste in processes
Understanding the importance of data and statistics in process improvement
Learning how to measure and analyze processes using statistical tools
Learning how to develop and implement improvement plans
Understanding how to sustain process improvements through effective control
Learning how to lead and facilitate process improvement projects
4.0 Course Contents

Day 1:

1. Introduction to Lean Six Sigma:

– Definition of Lean Six Sigma

– History of Lean Six Sigma

– Benefits of Lean Six Sigma

– Overview of the DMAIC (Define, Measure, Analyze, Improve, Control) methodology

2. Define Phase:

– Understanding customer requirements

– Developing project charter

– Defining project scope

– Identifying project team and stakeholders

– Defining project goals and objectives

3. Measure Phase:

– Collecting and analyzing data

– Defining metrics and KPIs

– Creating a baseline for performance

– Developing a measurement system

4. Analyze Phase:

– Identifying root causes of problems

– Conducting process mapping and value stream mapping

– Developing a cause-and-effect diagram (fishbone diagram)

– Conducting statistical analysis

5. Improve Phase:

– Generating and evaluating improvement ideas

– Developing and implementing solutions

– Conducting pilot tests

– Developing a change management plan

Day 2:

1. Control Phase:

– Developing a control plan

– Implementing a monitoring and measurement system

– Conducting regular process reviews

– Developing a continuous improvement plan

2. Lean Tools and Techniques:

– Understanding Lean principles

– Introduction to Lean tools such as value stream mapping, kaizen, and 5S

– Understanding the difference between Lean and Six Sigma

3. Six Sigma Tools and Techniques:

– Understanding Six Sigma principles

– Introduction to Six Sigma tools such as design of experiments (DOE), failure mode and effects analysis (FMEA), and statistical process control (SPC)

4. Implementing Lean Six Sigma:

– Developing a Lean Six Sigma implementation plan

– Identifying and addressing organizational barriers to Lean Six Sigma adoption

– Developing a Lean Six Sigma culture

5. Lean Six Sigma Culture:

   – Understanding the importance of culture in Lean Six Sigma

   – Developing a Lean Six Sigma culture

   – Encouraging and supporting Lean Six Sigma initiatives

   – Measuring and improving Lean Six Sigma culture

5.0 Target Audience

– Engineers, QASupervisors, Managers

6.0 Training Methodology & Certification

The training method will include a mix of lectures, interactive discussions, case studies, and hands-on exercises to ensure that participants gain a deep understanding of Lean Six Sigma principles and practices. The training will also provide opportunities for participants to network with other professionals in their field. Participants will receive a certificate of completion at the end of the course.
