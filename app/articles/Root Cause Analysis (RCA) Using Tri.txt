1.0: Title

Root Cause Analysis (RCA) Using Tripod Beta Method

HRDCorp Claimable Training Program Approval No: 10001360333


2.0: Introduction

Root Cause Analysis (RCA) Using Tripod Beta Method is a two-day program designed to provide participants with the necessary skills and knowledge to effectively implement the TCA using Tripod Beta methodology. The Tripod beta method is based on the Human Error theory. This theory states that incidents happen when people make errors and fail to keep the barriers functional or in place. These errors are Immediate Causes. The RCA Tripod Beta is a structured approach that focuses on identifying the root causes of problems and implementing corrective actions to prevent recurrence.

3.0: Learning Outcome

Upon completion of the program, participants should be able to:

Understand the principles and philosophy of RCA and its importance in improving safety, quality, and productivity.
Learn the Tripod Beta method, including its phases, tools, and techniques.
Develop skills in conducting effective RCA investigations using the Tripod Beta method.
Understand how to identify and classify root causes using the Tripod Beta method.
Learn how to develop and implement corrective actions using the Tripod Beta method.
Understand the importance of verification and validation of corrective actions.
Learn how to communicate RCA findings and recommendations effectively to stakeholders.
Develop a plan for implementing RCA in your organization using the Tripod Beta method.
4.0 Course Contents

Day 1:

1. Introduction to RCA and Tripod Beta methodology

2. Understanding the Tripod Beta process and tools

3. Identifying and defining the problem statement

4. Collecting and analyzing data

5. Developing a cause and effect diagram

6. Conducting a 5 Whys analysis

7. Identifying and prioritizing root causes

8. Developing corrective actions and implementing them

9. Developing an RCA report and presenting findings

Day 2:

1. Facilitating RCA meetings and developing RCA teams

2. Analyzing and interpreting data to support RCA findings

3. Communicating RCA results and recommendations to stakeholders

4. Implementing and monitoring corrective actions

5. Reviewing and updating the RCA process

6. Case studies and practical exercises

7. Evaluation and feedback
